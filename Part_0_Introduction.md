# The ApC's of Surgical Technology
## A Field Guide for the Working Scrub Tech

---

This book is not a textbook.

It is a reference built for the OR floor — for the tech who wants to get faster, get sharper, and stop getting caught off guard. Whether you are three months out of school or three years into your career, this book is written for the moment you are standing at a back table trying to figure out what comes next.

The framework is simple. Every surgery — every single one — has three phases:

---

> **THE APPROACH**
> How do you get in?
> — Position. Prep. Draping. Gown and gloves. The knife. Retractors.

> **THE PROCEDURE**
> What happens once you're inside?
> — Anatomy. Dissection. Steps. What the surgeon needs and when.

> **THE CLOSURE**
> How do you get back out?
> — Layer by layer. Specific suture for each layer. Drain or no drain. Dressing.

---

That structure — **Approach, Procedure, Closure** — never changes. The instruments change. The specialty changes. The patient changes. But any scrub tech who has internalized this framework can walk into a case they have never seen before, read the procedure, and immediately know what questions to ask.

## How to Use This Book

**Part I** covers the foundations — sterile technique, instrumentation, sutures, hemostasis, positioning, anesthesia. Read these when you need to understand the *why* behind what you do. They are written as practical guides, not lectures.

**Part II** is where you will spend most of your time. It covers every major surgical specialty, procedure by procedure. Each procedure is structured under the three ApC phases. At the top of every procedure you will find:

- **Quick Facts** — wound class, position, average time
- **Before You Scrub** — a checklist of what to confirm before the case starts
- The full **Approach → Procedure → Closure** breakdown
- **Watch For** — what goes wrong, what to anticipate, what the surgeon will call for that you may not expect

**Part III** covers closure principles, drains, dressings, specimen handling, and post-op transitions — the last phase of every case.

**Appendix G** has Mayo stand setups for every major procedure. **Appendix H** has one-page quick-reference cards — everything you need for a case on a single page.

## The Standard You Are Building Toward

The difference between a good scrub tech and an excellent one is anticipation. The excellent tech never makes the surgeon wait. They know what instrument is needed before it's asked for. They know when to pass and when to hold. They know the anatomy well enough to understand what just happened and what comes next.

That is not a personality trait. It is a skill built through deliberate study of how surgeries actually work — one procedure at a time, in the exact format this book uses.

Start with the cases you scrub most often. Read the procedure the night before. Check your Mayo stand setup against Appendix G. Walk through the Watch For section. Then scrub the case and see what you notice that you would have missed before.

That is how this book is meant to be used.

# Chapter 15: Neurosurgery

---

## Learning Objectives

By the end of this chapter, the learner will be able to:

1. Apply the ApC framework to cranial and spinal neurosurgical procedures.
2. Describe the specific draping, incision, and retractor setup for craniotomy and spinal surgery.
3. Explain the microsurgical principles and cottonoid management specific to neurosurgery.
4. Describe the VP shunt procedure using the ApC framework.

---

## 15.1 Neuroanatomy Overview

**Brain:** Cerebrum (frontal, parietal, temporal, occipital lobes), cerebellum, brainstem. Covered by dura mater → arachnoid → pia mater. CSF flows in the subarachnoid space.

**Spinal column:** 7 cervical, 12 thoracic, 5 lumbar, 5 fused sacral. Spinal cord ends at L1–L2 (conus medullaris). Below this: cauda equina.

**Critical structures always at risk:**
- **RLN/facial nerve (CN VII):** preserved during posterior fossa and neck procedures
- **Venous sinuses (sagittal, transverse, sigmoid):** laceration causes massive, rapidly fatal hemorrhage
- **Major cerebral arteries:** sacrifice causes stroke

---

## 15.2 Craniotomy

### The Approach

**Position:** Determined by the target brain region. Most common positions:
- **Supine** (head neutral or turned): frontal, temporal, parietal lesions
- **Lateral / park bench**: posterior fossa, cerebellopontine angle
- **Prone**: posterior fossa, occipital, posterior cervical
- Head fixed in **Mayfield three-pin skull clamp** for all cranial cases (rigid fixation to the OR table); pins placed into the outer skull table under sterile conditions.

**Prep:** Betadine or CHG/alcohol prep of the entire scalp (not CHG near ears). Scalp hair may be shaved along the planned incision line (narrow strip) or the surgeon may part the hair and mark without shaving.

**Draping:**
- **Head drape system**: head/turban drapes that wrap around the head and isolate the operative site
- Craniotomy aperture drape (adhesive-bordered opening) over the planned incision site
- The draping must not obstruct the anesthesia provider's access to the airway (at the head)
- A separate sterile setup for the microscope: the surgical microscope is draped with a **sterile microscope cover** before positioning over the field

**Gown and Gloves:** Standard. The scrub tech must accommodate the fact that the sterile field is at head level — the back table is positioned at the surgeon's side.

**The Knife:** #10 blade on a #3 handle for the scalp incision. The scalp is highly vascular — bleeding from the scalp incision is controlled with **Raney clips** (spring-loaded scalp clips applied along the cut edge immediately after the skin is opened to compress the diploic vessels). The scrub tech should have 20–40 Raney clips pre-loaded in the Raney clip applier.

**Retractors:**
- **Scalp:** Raney clips hold the skin edges open and control bleeding simultaneously
- **Deep:** Self-retaining brain retractors — the **Leyla bar / Greenberg retractor system** or **Sugita frame** provides adjustable, freestanding brain retraction without requiring an assistant to hold
- Individual brain retractors (flat, padded malleable retractors — covered with cottonoids) are placed on the brain surface and held by the retractor arm system
- The brain must never be retracted without a cottonoid between the retractor blade and the cortex

---

### The Procedure

1. Scalp incision (planned for the best cosmetic result and optimal access); Raney clips applied as the blade advances
2. Elevate scalp/pericranial flap; hold with fishhook retractors on rubber bands attached to the drape
3. **Burr holes:** high-speed drill with round cutting burr; 4–6 holes at corners of planned bone flap; irrigate continuously
4. **Craniotome cut:** footplate-tipped craniotome runs between burr holes, cutting the skull; the bone flap is elevated; epidural veins cauterized with bipolar; bone edges waxed
5. **Dural opening:** cruciate or curvilinear dural incision with a #15 blade; dural edges held open with sutures ("tenting" sutures) to the pericranium; brain surface now visible
6. Intracranial work (tumor, AVM, aneurysm): using operating microscope, bipolar forceps, brain retractors, cottonoids, CUSA (if tumor), micro-scissors
7. **Cottonoid management:** every cottonoid placed in the brain has a radiopaque thread and must be counted; placed to protect retracted cortex; all must be removed before closure
8. **Dural closure:** watertight; 4-0 Nurolon or Ti-cron; if insufficient dura for primary closure, duraplasty (DuraGen, autologous pericranium, or fascial graft)
9. **Bone flap replacement:** titanium plates (CranioFix, Synthes) and screws fix the bone flap back in anatomic position

---

### The Closure

**Galeal/periosteal layer:** 2-0 Vicryl interrupted; this layer provides strength to the scalp closure.

**Skin:** Staples (most efficient for scalp) or 3-0 nylon interrupted. Raney clips are removed as each segment is closed.

**Drain:** Subgaleal Jackson-Pratt drain (some surgeons place; some do not); brought out through a separate stab incision.

**Dressing:** Minimal — a light fluffy head wrap (Kerlix) provides pressure without excessive bulk. Do not use heavy dressings that obscure neurological assessment.

## 15.3 VP Shunt Placement

### The Approach

**Position:** Supine; head turned away from the shunt side (right-sided shunts are most common); neck slightly extended. The surgical site spans from the head to the abdomen — position must allow access to both.

**Prep:** Two fields prepped:
- **Head:** betadine or CHG; scalp prep around Kocher's point (2.5 cm from midline, 1 cm anterior to the coronal suture)
- **Abdomen:** CHG/alcohol; midline or right-upper-quadrant prep for the peritoneal end

**Draping:**
- Head drape framing the scalp burr hole site
- Separate abdominal aperture drape (or a single extended drape covering both sites with two openings)
- The neck and chest between the two sites must also be within the sterile field — the shunt tubing is tunneled subcutaneously this entire distance

**Gown and Gloves:** Standard; two scrubbed team members may be used if both sites are worked simultaneously.

**The Knife:** #15 blade on a #3 handle for the scalp incision (small, ~3 cm); #10 or #15 for the abdominal incision.

**Retractors:** Small self-retaining retractor (Weitlaner) in the scalp; small S-retractor or Richardson for the abdominal incision to the peritoneum.

---

### The Procedure

1. Scalp incision at Kocher's point; periosteum elevated; single burr hole drilled with perforating drill bit
2. Dural coagulation (bipolar) and small cruciate incision
3. Ventricular catheter inserted perpendicular to the brain surface (aiming for the inner canthus of the ipsilateral eye in the coronal plane; nasion in the sagittal plane) until CSF flows freely
4. Valve/reservoir connected to ventricular catheter; placed in a subgaleal pocket
5. **Tunneling:** a rigid tunneling rod is passed subcutaneously from the scalp incision, behind the ear, down the neck, across the chest, to the abdominal incision — the distal peritoneal catheter is threaded through the tunnel
6. Abdominal incision opened down to peritoneum; purse-string suture placed; peritoneum punctured; distal catheter inserted intraperitoneally (10–15 cm of catheter inside the peritoneum)
7. System connected; CSF flow confirmed by depressing the valve

---

### The Closure

**Scalp:** 2-0 Vicryl galeal layer; staples or 3-0 nylon for skin.

**Abdominal fascia:** 0 Vicryl interrupted.

**Abdominal subcutaneous:** 3-0 Vicryl.

**Abdominal skin:** Staples or 4-0 Monocryl.

**Dressings:** Gauze 4×4 at scalp and abdomen; light Kerlix head wrap.

## 15.4 Lumbar Laminectomy / Discectomy

### The Approach

**Position:** Prone on a **Wilson frame**, **Relton-Hall frame**, or chest rolls; hips and knees slightly flexed to reduce lumbar lordosis and open the interlaminar spaces; face in a padded horseshoe or Mayfield headrest.

**Prep:** CHG/alcohol; prep the lumbar back from T10 to the gluteal crease, full width.

**Draping:**
- Impervious draping towels to frame the midline lumbar incision (taped or stapled to skin)
- Adhesive incise drape (Ioban) placed over the prep site
- Fenestrated spine drape or a combination of split sheet + laparotomy sheet

**Gown and Gloves:** Standard.

**The Knife:** #10 blade on a #3 handle; midline longitudinal incision over the affected levels; typically 4–6 cm.

**Retractors:**
- **Initial:** small Army-Navy retractors to retract skin and subcutaneous tissue
- **Deep:** **Taylor retractors** or **McCulloch retractors** placed subperiosteally against the laminae and articular processes (holding the erector spinae muscles laterally)
- These are hand-held retractors; the assistant holds steady lateral retraction to maintain the laminectomy exposure throughout the case
- **For discectomy:** after laminotomy, a **pituitary rongeur** and **curette** work within the disc space; a **nerve root retractor** (Love nerve root retractor or ball-tipped nerve hook) protects the dural sac and nerve root while the disc is accessed

---

### The Procedure

1. Incise skin and thoracolumbar fascia; subperiosteal dissection of the paraspinal muscles off the spinous processes and laminae (Cobb elevator, periosteal elevator)
2. Insert retractors to hold muscles laterally
3. **Laminotomy/laminectomy:** remove ligamentum flavum with Kerrison punch (2, 3, 4 mm); remove inferior edge of the cephalad lamina and superior edge of the caudal lamina with Kerrison punch; create window to expose the dura
4. **Discectomy:** identify the disc herniation; gently retract the dural sac medially with nerve root retractor; incise the annulus fibrosus with a #15 blade; remove herniated disc material with pituitary rongeur (angled and straight); remove loose nuclear material until decompression is complete
5. Confirm nerve root decompression by passing a blunt probe into the neural foramen freely

---

### The Closure

**Fascia:** 1-0 Vicryl figure-of-eight interrupted; closes the thoracolumbar fascia over the midline.

**Subcutaneous:** 2-0 Vicryl.

**Skin:** Staples or 3-0 Monocryl.

**Drain:** Not routinely required for single-level discectomy. Used for multilevel laminectomy.

**Dressing:** Gauze 4×4 + ABD pad + waterproof tape (Tegaderm or Ioban to seal from wound drainage in prone patients).

## 15.5 Anterior Cervical Discectomy and Fusion (ACDF)

### The Approach

**Position:** Supine; small shoulder roll elevates the shoulders; neck slightly extended; head slightly turned away from the operative side (usually right-side approach).

**Prep:** CHG/alcohol; prep from chin to upper chest, lateral to both sternocleidomastoid muscles.

**Draping:** Four small towels framing the neck incision; head drape securing the hair; split sheet or thyroid-type drape exposing the anterior neck.

**Gown and Gloves:** Standard.

**The Knife:** #15 blade on a #3 handle; transverse incision in a skin crease at the appropriate cervical level (confirmed by palpating landmarks: hyoid = C3, thyroid cartilage = C4–C5, cricoid = C6).

**Retractors:**
- **Initial:** small army-navy retractors for skin and platysma
- **Deep (Smith-Robinson approach):** the carotid sheath (containing the carotid artery, IJV, and vagus nerve) is retracted laterally; the trachea and esophagus are retracted medially; a **Cloward or Caspar retractor system** provides self-retaining exposure of the anterior cervical spine
- **Distraction pins** (Caspar pins) inserted into the vertebral bodies above and below the disc level allow the disc space to be distracted open for work

---

### The Procedure

1. Incise platysma; develop the plane between carotid sheath laterally and midline structures medially (blunt, finger dissection)
2. Confirm level with a lateral fluoroscopic image (C-arm); a needle placed in the disc space confirms the target level
3. Remove anterior longitudinal ligament and disc material with curettes, pituitary rongeurs, and a high-speed burr
4. Distract the disc space with Caspar pins; remove posterior osteophytes (burr or Kerrison); confirm decompression (dural pulsation visible through the thinned posterior longitudinal ligament)
5. Measure disc space; select appropriate PEEK cage or bone graft spacer; impactor drives the cage into place; confirm position with fluoroscopy
6. Place anterior cervical plate; drill and place screws into the vertebral bodies above and below; lock screws; fluoroscopic confirmation

---

### The Closure

**Platysma:** 3-0 Vicryl interrupted.

**Subcutaneous:** 3-0 Vicryl.

**Skin:** 4-0 Monocryl subcuticular (cosmetically important in the neck).

**Drain:** Optional JP drain placed behind the platysma; some surgeons omit.

**Dressing:** Steri-strips over subcuticular closure; light gauze; cervical collar placed post-op.

---

## Clinical Pearls

> "In neurosurgery, the Approach demands absolute precision. The Raney clips, the Mayfield, the cottonoids — they are not accessories. They are the framework that makes everything that follows possible."

- When the craniotome is cutting, keep the irrigation running. Heat from the cut destroys bone at the edges and makes the flap harder to replace.
- Every cottonoid placed inside the skull must be accounted for before the dura is closed. Call them out by size when you place them; call them out when you remove them.
- In ACDF, the incise drape near the neck can lift with patient movement. Check its adhesion before the surgeon begins deep dissection.

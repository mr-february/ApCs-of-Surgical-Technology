# Chapter 14: Orthopedic Surgery

---

## Anatomy You Need at the Table

**Bone:** Cortical (dense outer shell) and cancellous (spongy inner). Long bones have a medullary canal. Bone heals by callus formation — 3–4 weeks cancellous, 6–8 weeks cortical.

**Joints:** Articular cartilage covers bone ends inside the joint. Ligaments = bone to bone. Tendons = muscle to bone. Synovial fluid lubricates.

**Why it matters at the back table:** Orthopedic cases run on fluoroscopy, implants, and power tools. Your job is to have the right implant trial ready before the surgeon asks, know the cement mixing sequence, and never let fluid contaminate the implant field.

## Total Hip Arthroplasty (THA)

> **QUICK FACTS**
> Wound Class: I (Clean) · Position: Lateral decubitus (posterior approach) or supine (anterior) · Avg time: 60–90 min · Team: surgeon + 1 assistant · Fluoroscopy: sometimes · Implants: acetabular shell + liner + femoral stem + head

### The Approach

> **BEFORE YOU SCRUB**
> - [ ] Confirm implant system and sizes templated — have 2 sizes above and below ready
> - [ ] Oscillating saw, reciprocating saw, and sagittal saw all available and tested
> - [ ] Trial components (femoral stems, heads, acetabular shells) pulled and on back table in order
> - [ ] Pulse lavage irrigator and bulb syringes ready
> - [ ] Cement: confirm type (if cemented stem); mixing supplies ready but do NOT mix until surgeon calls
> - [ ] Double-glove; have extra gloves your size on the field
> - [ ] Radiolucent table confirmed if anterior approach
> - [ ] Bovie pencil + bipolar available
> - [ ] Hemovac drain — confirm size and have suture (2-0 nylon) for securing

**Position:** Lateral decubitus, secured with beanbag and lateral post system. Dependent leg padded at knee and ankle. Axillary roll placed just below the axilla — not in it. Confirm stability before draping.

**Prep:** CHG/alcohol; entire lower extremity circumferentially, iliac crest to foot; include perineum (betadine) if visible.

**Draping:** Impervious stockinette over foot and lower leg → three-quarter sheet under the hip → Ioban adhesive incise drape over the hip → extremity (split sheet) wrap around the leg. Leg must hang free for rotation throughout the case.

**The Knife:** #10 blade on #3 handle. Posterior: incision from just posterior to the greater trochanter, curving proximally along the posterior femur, 10–15 cm.

**Retractors:** Cobra retractors for initial soft tissue. Sharp Hohmann retractors placed anterior, posterior, and inferior around the acetabular rim once the capsule is open. Offset Hohmann under the femoral neck during femoral preparation.

### The Procedure

1. Incise iliotibial band and gluteus maximus fascia; split gluteus maximus bluntly
2. Identify and tag the short external rotators (piriformis, obturator internus); divide near femoral insertion
3. Open and excise the posterior hip capsule; dislocate the femoral head anteriorly
4. Resect the femoral head with oscillating saw at the templated neck cut angle
5. **Acetabular prep:** place Hohmanns for 360° exposure; ream concentrically in increasing sizes until subchondral "bleeding bone" is uniformly exposed; impact acetabular shell (press-fit ± screws); insert liner
6. **Femoral prep:** sequentially broach the femoral canal in increasing sizes to cortical contact; trial stem + head; reduce hip; assess stability and ROM
7. Place definitive stem; attach definitive femoral head; reduce; final stability check

> **⚠ WATCH FOR**
> - **Trial head won't reduce:** surgeon needs a longer neck trial — have +0, +4, +8 mm options ready
> - **Acetabular reamer binds:** irrigate with bulb syringe; have backup reamers one size smaller in reach
> - **Cement mixing call:** once the surgeon calls for cement, you have a finite working window (8–10 min at room temp) — do not start mixing until that call; communicate time elapsed
> - **Glove perforation:** double-glove is standard; change outer gloves at implant placement if contamination is suspected
> - **Hohmann retractor slippage:** the posterior capsule will pull into the field — reposition immediately; do not let the surgeon work blind

### The Closure

| Layer | Suture | Notes |
|-------|--------|-------|
| External rotators (piriformis/obturator internus) | #1 Ethibond | Reattach through drill holes or suture anchors in femur |
| Fascia lata / IT band | #1 Vicryl | Interrupted or figure-of-eight |
| Subcutaneous | 2-0 Vicryl | |
| Skin | Staples or 3-0 Monocryl | Subcuticular for Monocryl |
| Drain | Hemovac through separate stab | Secure with 2-0 nylon |

**Dressing:** Gauze 4×4 + ABD pad + Elastikon or waterproof dressing.

## Total Knee Arthroplasty (TKA)

> **QUICK FACTS**
> Wound Class: I (Clean) · Position: Supine, tourniquet on proximal thigh · Avg time: 75–105 min · Team: surgeon + 1 assistant · Fluoroscopy: sometimes · Implants: femoral component + tibial tray + poly insert ± patellar button

### The Approach

> **BEFORE YOU SCRUB**
> - [ ] Confirm implant system, sizing, and whether patella will be resurfaced
> - [ ] Trial components (femur, tibia, poly inserts, patella button) pulled and ordered on back table
> - [ ] Oscillating saw + sagittal saw available; extra blades on field
> - [ ] Tourniquet: confirm pressure setting with surgeon (typically 250–300 mmHg for lower extremity) and inflate/deflate call
> - [ ] Cement: mixing supplies ready; do NOT mix until surgeon's call
> - [ ] Pulsatile lavage available for cement prep
> - [ ] Double-glove; replacement outer gloves on the field

**Position:** Supine; tourniquet on proximal thigh over cast padding. Lateral post or triangular bolster under the ipsilateral hip/thigh. Knee must be able to flex to 90°+ freely.

**Prep:** Elevate leg; CHG/alcohol circumferentially from tourniquet to toes; lay limb down.

**Draping:** Impervious stockinette over foot → split sheet below knee → Ioban incise drape → extremity drape wrapped below tourniquet. Confirm the knee can flex freely to 90° inside the drapes before proceeding.

**The Knife:** #10 blade on #3 handle. Anterior midline incision, 5 cm above the patella to the tibial tubercle, ~12–15 cm. Made with the knee in 90° flexion.

**Retractors:** Medial parapatellar arthrotomy with Hohmann retractors placed medially, laterally, and posteriorly within the joint. Patella is everted or subluxed laterally. Posterior Hohmann (or dedicated knee retractor) protects the posterior capsule during tibial cut.

### The Procedure

1. Medial parapatellar arthrotomy; enter knee joint; evert/sublux patella
2. Partial removal of anterior fat pad; identify landmarks
3. **Distal femoral cut:** distal cutting guide pinned; oscillating saw
4. **AP, posterior, and chamfer cuts:** anterior/posterior/chamfer guide pinned; oscillating saw; femoral box cut if posterior-stabilized component used
5. **Tibial cut:** extra- or intramedullary guide; oscillating saw perpendicular to tibial shaft axis
6. **Trial:** trial femur + tibial tray + poly insert; flex/extend knee; balance flexion and extension gaps; check medial/lateral balance
7. **Patellar resurfacing (if performed):** saw guide, drill holes, trial button, cement definitive button
8. **Cement and final components:** cement femoral component; cement tibial tray; insert poly; hold all components fully pressurized until cement cures
9. Deflate tourniquet; control bleeding before closing
10. Final ROM check

> **⚠ WATCH FOR**
> - **Gap imbalance:** surgeon will need different poly thickness — have thin and thick inserts immediately available
> - **Cement timing:** track time from mix to implant; alert the surgeon at 5 minutes elapsed so they can complete seating before the cement sets
> - **Tourniquet time:** communicate elapsed tourniquet time at 90-minute mark; cumulative ischemia risk increases after 2 hours
> - **Patella eversion resistance:** if knee is tight, surgeon may need a lateral retinacular release — have Metzenbaum scissors and bipolar ready
> - **Irrigation flooding:** saline runs heavily during pulsatile lavage — protect your back table edge with a barrier drape

### The Closure

| Layer | Suture | Notes |
|-------|--------|-------|
| Arthrotomy (quadriceps/medial parapatellar) | #1 Vicryl | Interrupted figure-of-eight; critical layer |
| Subcutaneous | 2-0 Vicryl | |
| Skin | Staples or 3-0 Monocryl | |
| Drain | Hemovac through lateral stab | Surgeon preference varies |

**Dressing:** Saline-moistened Kerlix around knee → cast padding → ACE wrap from foot to mid-thigh.

## ORIF — Distal Radius Fracture

> **QUICK FACTS**
> Wound Class: I (Clean) · Position: Supine, arm on hand table · Avg time: 45–75 min · Team: surgeon + 1 assistant · Fluoroscopy: required (mini C-arm) · Implants: volar locking plate (VLP) + screws

### The Approach

> **BEFORE YOU SCRUB**
> - [ ] Radiolucent hand table confirmed; mini C-arm sterile drape on field
> - [ ] VLP system: confirm plate size (templated on X-ray); have one size up/down available
> - [ ] Locking screw set with depth gauge and drill bits specific to the plate system
> - [ ] Tourniquet on proximal forearm; confirm it's applied before prep
> - [ ] K-wires available for temporary fixation during reduction
> - [ ] Reduction forceps (periosteal elevator, Weber clamps) on the field

**Position:** Supine; arm fully extended on a radiolucent hand table. Tourniquet on proximal forearm.

**Prep:** CHG/alcohol; entire arm from tourniquet to fingertips circumferentially.

**Draping:** Impervious stockinette to the fingers → split sheet under the arm → extremity drape → sterile mini C-arm drape over the imaging detector.

**The Knife:** #15 blade on #3 handle. Volar approach (Henry): longitudinal incision along the volar radial wrist, 5–7 cm.

**Retractors:** Army-Navy retractors initially; small Weitlaner in subcutaneous layer; small Richardson retractors to protect flexor tendons and neurovascular structures during fracture exposure.

### The Procedure

1. Incise skin and subcutaneous; identify FCR tendon; retract FCR radially, FPL ulnarly
2. Incise floor of FCR sheath; expose pronator quadratus (PQ)
3. Incise PQ off the radial border; elevate subperiosteally to expose the fracture
4. Reduce fracture fragments; hold with K-wires or reduction forceps; confirm fluoroscopically
5. Apply volar locking plate; confirm position with fluoroscopy
6. Drill; measure; place locking screws distally, cortical screws in the shaft
7. Final fluoroscopic images in multiple planes; confirm alignment and hardware

> **⚠ WATCH FOR**
> - **Depth gauge misread:** always confirm measurement before handing the screw; a screw too long violates the dorsal cortex and irritates extensor tendons
> - **Fluoroscopy call:** surgeon will call "fluoro" — clear your hands and everything metal from the field immediately
> - **K-wire removal timing:** temporary K-wires come out before the plate is finalized — don't lose them; account for them in the count
> - **FCR sheath retraction:** if the retractor slips and FCR gets pulled into the field, the surgeon will stop — reposition and hold steady

### The Closure

| Layer | Suture | Notes |
|-------|--------|-------|
| Pronator quadratus | 3-0 Vicryl | Provides soft tissue coverage over the plate |
| FCR sheath floor | 3-0 Vicryl | |
| Subcutaneous | 3-0 Vicryl | |
| Skin | 4-0 Monocryl subcuticular or interrupted nylon | Steri-strips over |

**Splint:** Volar plaster or fiberglass splint applied in OR. Webril padding under the splint; ACE wrap.

## Knee Arthroscopy with Partial Meniscectomy

> **QUICK FACTS**
> Wound Class: I (Clean) · Position: Supine, leg in leg holder at table break · Avg time: 20–45 min · Team: surgeon only or + 1 · Fluoroscopy: rarely · Implants: none (meniscectomy); suture anchors if repair

### The Approach

> **BEFORE YOU SCRUB**
> - [ ] Arthroscopy tower: camera, light source, shaver console, fluid pump — confirm all are on and functioning before draping
> - [ ] 30° arthroscope loaded and white-balanced with camera
> - [ ] Shaver blades: confirm type (full-radius resector or biter); have one backup
> - [ ] Irrigation fluid: 3L saline bags spiked and primed in the pump tubing; have at least 2 bags on the field
> - [ ] Arthroscopic basket forceps (biter) in multiple orientations: straight, left, right — all three
> - [ ] Arthroscopic probe on the field
> - [ ] If repair is possible: meniscal repair implants (all-inside system) pulled and ready
> - [ ] Fluid collection pouch drape under the knee before final draping

**Position:** Supine; leg in leg holder (circumferential post at table break); tourniquet on proximal thigh (inflate only if visibility is poor).

**Prep:** CHG/alcohol; entire lower leg and knee from tourniquet to ankle circumferentially.

**Draping:** Impervious stockinette to the ankle → extremity drape → split sheet below tourniquet → fluid collection pouch drape under knee.

**The Knife:** #11 blade on #3 handle (stab incisions only). Anterolateral portal: 1 cm, just lateral to the patellar tendon at the joint line. Anteromedial portal: mirror image medially.

**Retractors:** None — fluid pump pressure distends the joint and provides the working space.

### The Procedure

1. Inject local anesthetic into portal sites; stab incisions with #11 blade
2. Insert arthroscope trocar-cannula; remove trocar; attach camera; begin irrigation and distend joint
3. Systematic inspection of all compartments: suprapatellar pouch → medial gutter → medial compartment → intercondylar notch (ACL/PCL) → lateral compartment → lateral gutter → patellofemoral joint
4. Identify meniscal tear; classify type, location, zone (red-red / red-white / white-white)
5. Probe the tear through the anteromedial portal to assess stability and extent
6. Switch arthroscope to anteromedial portal; place biter/shaver in anterolateral portal
7. Resect unstable meniscal fragment with biter or motorized shaver; contour the remaining rim smoothly
8. Irrigate joint; remove all loose fragments; inspect final result

> **⚠ WATCH FOR**
> - **Fluid extravasation:** if the pump pressure is too high or a capsular tear exists, the knee will swell dramatically — monitor with surgeon; drop pump pressure if swelling is noted around the joint
> - **Shaver suction clog:** small meniscal fragments clog the shaver — have a syringe of saline to back-flush; keep a spare blade available
> - **Tear bigger than expected:** surgeon may pivot to meniscal repair mid-case — have the all-inside repair system unsealed and on the back table before the case starts
> - **Portal instrument crowding:** with both portals occupied, passing instruments requires hand-off coordination — stay alert and pass only what's called
> - **Irrigation runoff:** saline runs constantly; your back table will be wet — keep sharps and loose items elevated and secure

### The Closure

| Layer | Suture | Notes |
|-------|--------|-------|
| Portal sites | 4-0 nylon interrupted × 1 per portal | Or skin glue |

No deep layer closure — portals are too small to require it.

**Dressing:** 4×4 gauze over portals → cotton roll or Kerlix around knee → ACE wrap.

## Clinical Pearls

> "In orthopedics, the Approach is also mechanical. A retractor that slips or an incise drape that peels mid-case compromises the sterile field around a permanent implant."

- Arthroscopy runs wet. Put a waterproof barrier between your back table and the field before the irrigation pump starts.
- Cement has a working window. Warm rooms shorten it. Track elapsed time from mix and tell the surgeon at 5 minutes — don't wait to be asked.
- Double-glove for every joint arthroplasty. Glove perforation rates run 20–30% in TJA. The inner glove is the implant's last line of defense.
- Fluoroscopy is called without warning. Keep your hands out of the beam when the C-arm comes in — establish a habit of stepping back the moment the machine moves.

    Appendix F: Study Questions by Chapter¶

    Use these questions for self-assessment, group study, or examination preparation. Answers are found within each respective chapter.

    Part I: The Approach¶

    Chapter 1 — What Is Surgical Technology?¶

    1. What does CST stand for, and which organization administers the exam?
    2. What is the AST, and what is its purpose?
    3. What year was the Association of Surgical Technologists founded?
    4. Describe the three phases of the surgical experience.
    5. What is the difference between the scrub role and the circulating role?
    6. Define "scope of practice."
    7. What is HIPAA, and how does it affect the surgical technologist?
    8. Define surgical conscience.
    9. How many CE credits are required every four years for CST recertification?
    10. Name three ethical obligations of a CST.

    Chapter 2 — The Operating Room Environment¶

    1. Describe the three OR traffic zones and the attire required in each.
    2. What does "positive pressure" mean in the context of the OR?
    3. What is the minimum number of air exchanges per hour in an OR?
    4. What is laminar airflow and where is it used?
    5. What is the OR temperature range and why is it maintained there?
    6. Describe the fire triad and give an OR example of each element.
    7. What is the RACE protocol?
    8. Describe the difference between the back table and the Mayo stand.
    9. What are Raney clips used for?
    10. Why should OR doors remain closed during active cases?

    Chapter 3 — Microbiology and Infection Control¶

    1. Define pathogen and give two examples of gram-positive and two gram-negative bacteria.
    2. What is a spore, and why is it significant to sterilization?
    3. Describe the chain of infection and its six links.
    4. What are the three modes of transmission, and which is most relevant to SSI?
    5. What is the difference between standard precautions and transmission-based precautions?
    6. Why is alcohol-based hand rub ineffective against C. difficile?
    7. Describe the surgical hand scrub procedure.
    8. What is the neutral zone and why is it used?
    9. What is biofilm and why does it matter in instrument processing?
    10. Name three risk factors for SSI in the surgical patient.

    Chapter 4 — Sterilization and Disinfection¶

    1. Define sterilization and differentiate it from disinfection.
    2. Describe the Spaulding classification and provide examples for each category.
    3. What are the six steps in the instrument reprocessing workflow?
    4. Compare steam sterilization and EtO: indications, advantages, disadvantages.
    5. What is IUSS and when is it appropriate?
    6. Describe the difference between a biological indicator and a chemical indicator.
    7. What organism is used in a BI for steam sterilization?
    8. What action is taken when a BI returns positive?
    9. Explain event-related sterility.
    10. Why must instruments be cleaned before sterilization?

    Chapter 5 — The Sterile Field¶

    1. What is the sterile field? List five items that are part of it.
    2. What does "strike-through contamination" mean?
    3. List ten principles of aseptic technique.
    4. Describe closed gloving technique.
    5. How should a circulator transfer a peel-pouch item to the sterile field?
    6. What is surgical conscience, and how does it relate to contamination?
    7. When is open gloving technique used?
    8. Why should the sterile field not be left unattended?
    9. Describe how to detect and respond to a contamination event.
    10. What is the rule about repositioning placed drapes?

    Chapter 6 — Surgical Instrumentation¶

    1. Name the five categories of instruments and give two examples of each.
    2. What is the difference between a Mayo and a Metzenbaum scissor?
    3. How is a scalpel blade safely mounted?
    4. Describe how to pass a loaded needle holder.
    5. When are counts performed?
    6. What is the procedure when a final count does not reconcile?
    7. What is a preference card?
    8. Describe the proper technique for passing a free tie.
    9. What is a Kittner/peanut, and how is it prepared?
    10. What happens if the instrument count is unresolved when the surgeon wants to close?

    Chapter 7 — Sutures, Needles, and Wound Closure Devices¶

    1. Differentiate absorbable and non-absorbable suture.
    2. Compare monofilament and braided suture.
    3. What does "3-0" mean in suture sizing?
    4. Is 2-0 larger or smaller than 3-0? Explain why.
    5. Name three absorbable sutures and their approximate absorption times.
    6. Describe three needle point types and a use for each.
    7. What position on the needle should suture be loaded in the needle holder?
    8. What is a GIA stapler, and how does it differ from an EEA?
    9. When is tissue adhesive (Dermabond) appropriate?
    10. What is a retention suture, and when is it indicated?

    Chapter 8 — Hemostasis¶

    1. Describe the three phases of physiologic hemostasis.
    2. How is a free tie passed to the surgeon?
    3. What is the difference between monopolar and bipolar electrosurgery?
    4. Why must the dispersive electrode be applied correctly?
    5. Name three topical hemostatic agents and describe each.
    6. Why must thrombin never be injected IV?
    7. Name three laser-related hazards and their controls.
    8. What is the Argon beam coagulator?
    9. What does ESU stand for, and what does it do?
    10. Describe the scrub tech's proactive role in anticipating hemorrhage.

    Chapter 9 — Anesthesia¶

    1. What are the four A's of general anesthesia?
    2. Describe the difference between spinal and epidural anesthesia.
    3. Name two IV induction agents and one key characteristic of each.
    4. What is the earliest sign of malignant hyperthermia?
    5. Name the two categories of anesthetic agent that trigger MH.
    6. What is dantrolene, and what is the initial dose?
    7. Why is epinephrine contraindicated in digital anesthesia?
    8. What does CRNA stand for?
    9. Describe the five phases of general anesthesia.
    10. What is the MHAUS hotline number for MH emergencies?

    Chapter 10 — Patient Positioning¶

    1. What are the five goals of surgical positioning?
    2. Identify pressure points in the supine position and associated risks.
    3. How many people are needed to safely turn a patient prone?
    4. What is the purpose and correct placement of the axillary roll?
    5. What is compartment syndrome, and which position increases its risk?
    6. What is the Fowler's position complication called venous air embolism?
    7. Why must both legs be raised/lowered simultaneously in lithotomy?
    8. Name two positioning considerations specific to obese patients.
    9. What is maximum safe tourniquet inflation time for the lower extremity?
    10. Describe the jackknife position and its surgical indication.

    Chapter 11 — Skin Preparation and Draping¶

    1. What is the purpose of surgical skin prep?
    2. Compare CHG and povidone-iodine.
    3. Why are razors prohibited for preoperative hair removal?
    4. Where do you start skin prep, and in which direction do you work?
    5. What is the rule about prepping a contaminated area?
    6. What is the rule about repositioning placed drapes?
    7. What is an incise drape, and what must be true before applying it?
    8. Why must alcohol-based preps dry completely before draping?
    9. What is the umbilicus prepped with and why?
    10. Which prep agent is used for vaginal and perineal prep?

    Part II: The Procedure¶

    Chapter 12 — Wound Classification and Tissue Handling¶

    1. Describe the four wound classifications with one example each.
    2. What is the SSI rate for a Class IV wound?
    3. Describe primary, secondary, and tertiary wound healing.
    4. What is traction and countertraction?
    5. Describe the five principles of tissue handling.
    6. What is a Kittner, and how is it used?
    7. List the layers of the anterior abdominal wall from superficial to deep.
    8. What is the linea alba?
    9. What is hydrodissection?
    10. What is blunt dissection and when is it preferred?

    (Study questions continue for Chapters 13–26 — one set of 10 per chapter — covering all specialty content in Part II and all closure content in Part III. These questions are distributed throughout the text at the end of each chapter.)

    Comprehensive Review: The ApC's at a Glance¶

    Quick-Reference Questions for Exam Prep¶

    On the Approach: - What are the three OR zones? - What temperature is maintained in an OR? - Name five principles of aseptic technique. - What is a Class 5 chemical indicator? - What is the gold standard for monitoring sterilization cycles? - Name three types of drapes. - What is the Critical View of Safety?

    On the Procedure: - What is the most common SSI pathogen? - What is malignant hyperthermia and its antidote? - Name three types of surgical hemostasis. - What is the difference between TAPP and TEP hernia repair? - Name the three types of bowel anastomosis. - What is the TURP syndrome and how is it prevented? - What is cottonoid management in neurosurgery? - Name the components of a total hip arthroplasty.

    On the Closure: - What is dead space, and how is it eliminated? - Name four types of wound drains. - What is event-related sterility? - What is NPWT (wound VAC)? - When must frozen section specimens NOT be placed in formalin? - What documentation is required for implants? - What is the SBAR framework? - What is wound dehiscence, and what does "salmon-colored drainage" indicate?

    Appendix D: Surgical Positions Reference¶

    Position Description Common Procedures Key Pressure Points Primary Complications
    Supine Flat on back; arms at side or on arm boards Abdominal, cardiac, lower extremity, head/neck Occiput, scapulae, olecranon, sacrum, heels Brachial plexus (arm hyperabduction), ulnar nerve (elbow), heel pressure
    Prone Face down; chest rolls; head in headrest Posterior spine, posterior cranial, rectal, posterior extremity Forehead, breasts (women), genitalia (men), iliac crests, knees, dorsum of feet Airway/ETT, ischemic optic neuropathy, abdominal compression, brachial plexus
    Lateral On side; axillary roll; arm sled Lung, kidney, lateral hip arthroplasty Ear, dependent shoulder, ribs, iliac crest, medial knee, dependent ankle Axillary bundle injury, peroneal nerve, positional instability
    Lithotomy Supine with hips flexed, legs in stirrups Vaginal, perineal, rectal, urologic, laparoscopic GYN Sacrum, popliteal fossa (peroneal nerve), heels, ankles Compartment syndrome, peroneal nerve palsy, hip dislocation
    Trendelenburg Head down, feet elevated Lower abdominal/pelvic, laparoscopic, CV line placement Standard supine points; shoulder if braces used Increased ICP/IOP, brachial plexus (if shoulder braces), respiratory compromise
    Reverse Trendelenburg Head up, feet down Upper abdominal, laparoscopic upper GI, thyroid, bariatric Heels, standard supine + footboard Hypotension, footboard pressure
    Fowler's / Beach Chair Semi-sitting or fully upright (30°–90°) Shoulder arthroscopy, craniotomy, ENT Ischial tuberosities, coccyx, heels, occiput Venous air embolism, hypotension, cervical hyperextension
    Jackknife (Kraske) Prone with table flexed; hips highest point Anal, rectal, pilonidal Same as prone; knee pressure at table break Same as prone; abdominal compression at flex point

    Positioning Accessories Reference¶

    Equipment Primary Use Notes
    Arm board Support arm in supine; abduction ≤90° Pad thoroughly; never abduct >90°
    Allen / Yellow Fin stirrups Lithotomy leg support Distributes weight along thigh; most protective
    Candy cane stirrups Lithotomy Older design; more stress on ankle/hip
    Knee crutch stirrups Lithotomy Supports knee; allows leg extension
    Bean bag positioner Lateral stability Vacuum-hardened; custom molds to patient
    Chest rolls / bolsters Prone; allows chest expansion Position under lateral chest, not on breasts
    Axillary roll Lateral; protects dependent axilla Placed just distal (caudal) to axilla, NOT in axilla
    Kidney rest (flank rest) Lateral; elevates kidney/flank Used with table break for maximal flank exposure
    Mayfield pins Rigid head fixation; cranial and cervical cases Local anesthetic to pin sites
    Wilson frame Prone; flexes lumbar spine Used for lumbar disc surgery
    Laminectomy frame (Relton-Hall) Prone spine; 4-post Supports chest and iliac crests; abdomen hangs free
    Safety strap Prevents patient from sliding/falling Placed 2 inches above knees; not restrictively tight
    Gel pad (standard) Padding for any bony prominence Various sizes; wipe clean between cases
    Egg crate / foam padding Pressure distribution Used for extremities, heels, occiput

    Positioning Nerve Injury Reference¶

    Nerve Position at Risk Prevention
    Brachial plexus Supine (arm hyperabduction), lateral, prone Arm board ≤90°; pad shoulder; avoid arm hyperextension
    Ulnar nerve Supine (elbow pressure on table) Pad elbow; arm board; check medial elbow padding
    Radial nerve Lateral (arm over edge) Pad lateral upper arm; arm sled
    Common peroneal nerve Lithotomy (fibular head on stirrup) Slide fingers behind knee; pad popliteal fossa
    Femoral nerve Lithotomy (excessive hip flexion) Limit hip flexion; no extreme stirrup height
    Sciatic nerve Lithotomy; prone Limit hip flexion and external rotation; pad
    Axillary vessels/brachial plexus Lateral (no axillary roll, or incorrect placement) Axillary roll just distal to axilla
    Ischemic optic neuropathy (ION) Prone (periocular pressure) Horseshoe or Mayfield headrest; eyes free; check pressure q30min

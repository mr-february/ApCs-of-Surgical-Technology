# Appendix E: Sterilization Methods Comparison

---

## Sterilization Methods Summary

| Method | Agent | Temperature | Time | Packaging | Compatible Materials | Not Compatible | BI Organism |
|--------|-------|------------|------|-----------|---------------------|---------------|------------|
| **Steam (Gravity)** | Saturated steam | 250°F (121°C) | 15–30 min | Woven/non-woven wrap, peel pouches, rigid containers | Stainless steel, most metals, glass, some plastics, rubber | Heat-sensitive items, delicate optics, electronics | *Geobacillus stearothermophilus* |
| **Steam (Pre-vacuum/Prevac)** | Saturated steam | 270°F (132°C) | 4–10 min | Same as gravity | Same as gravity | Same as gravity | Same as gravity |
| **IUSS (Flash)** | Steam | 270°F (132°C) | 3–10 min (unwrapped) | None (unwrapped) | Metal instruments | Implants (routine); anything that should be stored | *G. stearothermophilus* |
| **Ethylene Oxide (EtO)** | EtO gas | 55°C–60°C | 2–6 hrs (+ 8–12 hr aeration) | Gas-permeable pouches; must allow gas penetration | Heat-sensitive items, electronics, cameras, plastics | Aqueous solutions; requires aeration (toxic) | *Bacillus atrophaeus* |
| **Hydrogen Peroxide Plasma (Sterrad)** | H₂O₂ plasma | 45°C–55°C | 24–75 min | Specific non-cellulose materials (Tyvek pouches) | Cameras, powered instruments, flexible scopes, electronics | Cellulose (paper, linen), liquids, very long lumens | *G. stearothermophilus* |
| **Peracetic Acid (Steris)** | Peracetic acid + H₂O₂ | 50°C–56°C | ~30 min | None (immersion; no packaging) | Immersible instruments, flexible scopes (some) | Items that cannot be submerged; no storage after processing | *G. stearothermophilus* |
| **Dry Heat** | Hot air | 160°C–180°C | 1–2 hrs | Non-moisture-permeable containers (glass, metal trays) | Anhydrous materials, oils, powders, glassware | Most plastics, moisture-sensitive items | *Bacillus atrophaeus* |

## Chemical Indicator (CI) Classes

| Class | Type | Detects |
|-------|------|--------|
| Class 1 | Process indicator | Exposure to sterilization process (not parameters) |
| Class 2 | Specific-use indicator | Specific test (Bowie-Dick); air removal in pre-vac steam |
| Class 3 | Single-parameter indicator | One critical parameter |
| Class 4 | Multi-parameter indicator | Two or more parameters |
| Class 5 | Integrating indicator | All critical parameters; highest CI performance |
| Class 6 | Emulating indicator | All critical parameters for a specific cycle |

## Spaulding Classification Quick Reference

| Category | Device Contact | Minimum Processing | Examples |
|----------|---------------|-------------------|---------|
| **Critical** | Sterile tissue or vascular system | **Sterilization** | Surgical instruments, implants, IV catheters |
| **Semi-critical** | Intact mucous membranes or non-intact skin | **High-Level Disinfection** (sterilization preferred) | Flexible endoscopes, laryngoscope blades |
| **Non-critical** | Intact skin only | **Low- to intermediate-level disinfection** | BP cuffs, ECG leads, stethoscopes |

## High-Level Disinfectants

| Agent | Contact Time | Notes |
|-------|-------------|-------|
| **Glutaraldehyde (Cidex)** | 20–45 min (HLD); 10 hr (sterilization) | Toxic fumes; requires ventilation; must be rinsed |
| **Ortho-phthalaldehyde (OPA / Cidex OPA)** | 12 min at 20°C | Less irritating than glutaraldehyde; stains proteins (including skin) |
| **Peracetic acid (Steris, Medivator)** | 12 min (processing system) | Fast; effective; clean rinse cycle included |
| **Hydrogen peroxide (3.7–35%)** | Varies | Used in automated systems |
| **Sodium hypochlorite (bleach)** | Varies by concentration | HLD at high concentration; sporicidal; corrosive to metals |

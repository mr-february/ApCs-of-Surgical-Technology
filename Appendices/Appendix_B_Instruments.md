# Appendix B: Common Surgical Instruments Reference Guide

---

## Cutting and Dissecting Instruments

| Instrument | Category | Description | Use |
|-----------|---------|-------------|-----|
| Scalpel #3 handle | Cutting | Accepts blades 10, 11, 12, 15 | General incision |
| Scalpel #4 handle | Cutting | Accepts blades 20–25 (heavier blades) | Abdominal, orthopedic |
| Scalpel #7 handle | Cutting | Long, thin; accepts small blades | Delicate dissection |
| Blade #10 | Cutting | Large curved blade | General incision |
| Blade #11 | Cutting | Triangular/pointed tip | Stab wounds, incision of cysts |
| Blade #15 | Cutting | Small curved | Delicate dissection, pediatric, plastic |
| Blade #20 | Cutting | Heavy curved | Abdominal, orthopedic |
| Mayo scissors (straight) | Cutting | Heavy; straight | Cutting fascia, suture |
| Mayo scissors (curved) | Cutting | Heavy; curved | Tissue dissection |
| Metzenbaum scissors (curved) | Dissecting | Fine; curved | Delicate tissue dissection |
| Iris scissors | Cutting | Tiny; spring scissors | Ophthalmic, microsurgery |
| Suture scissors | Cutting | Blunt tips; short blades | Cutting suture only |
| Bandage scissors | Cutting | Angled lower blade | Non-sterile use; cast/dressing removal |

## Grasping and Clamping Instruments

| Instrument | Category | Description | Use |
|-----------|---------|-------------|-----|
| Adson forceps | Tissue forceps | Small; 1×2 teeth | Skin handling |
| Thumb (tissue) forceps | Tissue forceps | 1×2 teeth | General tissue grasping |
| Russian forceps | Tissue forceps | Rounded serrated jaw | General tissue |
| DeBakey forceps | Tissue forceps | Fine atraumatic serrations | Vascular, bowel |
| Smooth (dressing) forceps | Tissue forceps | No teeth | Dressings, packing |
| Mosquito hemostat | Hemostat | Very small; straight or curved | Tiny vessels |
| Kelly hemostat | Hemostat | Medium; straight or curved | Medium vessels |
| Crile hemostat | Hemostat | Similar to Kelly; full-length serrations | General hemostasis |
| Rochester-Ochsner / Kocher clamp | Hemostat | Heavy; toothed tip | Dense tissue, fascia |
| Allis clamp | Grasping | Multiple interlocking teeth | Tissue edges to be excised |
| Babcock clamp | Grasping | Smooth; fenestrated jaw | Bowel, tube, ureter (atraumatic) |
| Pennington clamp | Grasping | Triangular fenestrated jaw | Rectal, bowel tissue |
| Sponge forceps / Ring forceps | Grasping | Ring jaws; long handles | Sponge holding; skin prep |
| Towel clamp (Backhaus) | Securing | Non-perforating; sharp tips | Secure drapes (non-perforating) |
| Mayo-Hegar needle holder | Holding | Heavy; standard | General closure |
| Webster needle holder | Holding | Fine; shorter | Delicate closure |
| Castroviejo needle holder | Holding | Spring-action; very fine | Ophthalmic, microsurgery |

    Appendix C: Suture Quick Reference Chart¶

    Absorbable Sutures¶

    Brand Name Generic Name Structure Absorption Tensile Retention Common Uses
    Plain gut Collagen (bovine/ovine) Mono/multi 7–10 days Lost by day 7–10 Mucosa, ligation
    Chromic gut Chromated collagen Mono 10–21 days Lost by day 14–21 GI, GYN, mucosa
    Dexon Polyglycolic acid Braided 60–90 days 65% at 2 wk; 35% at 3 wk Fascia, subcutaneous
    Vicryl Polyglactin 910 Braided 56–70 days 75% at 2 wk; 50% at 3 wk Fascia, GI, subcutaneous
    Vicryl Rapide Polyglactin 910 (irradiated) Braided 10–14 days Lost by day 10–14 Skin, mucosa, episiotomy
    Monocryl Poliglecaprone 25 Mono 91–119 days 60% at 1 wk; 0% at 3 wk Subcuticular, dermis
    PDS II Polydioxanone Mono >180 days 70% at 2 wk; 50% at 4 wk Fascial closure, pediatric
    Biosyn Glycomer 631 Mono 90–110 days 75% at 2 wk General soft tissue
    Maxon Polyglyconate Mono 180 days 75% at 2 wk Fascia

    Non-Absorbable Sutures¶

    Brand Name Generic Name Structure Characteristics Common Uses
    Silk Silk Braided Excellent handling; high capillarity; loses tensile strength over years Vessel ties, cardiac, GI
    Ethilon / Dermalon Nylon Mono High memory; strong; degrades slowly in tissue Skin, ophthalmic, neuro
    Nurolon Nylon Braided Better handling than mono; lower memory Closure
    Prolene / Surgilene Polypropylene Mono Very smooth; minimal reaction; excellent vascular use Vascular anastomosis, hernia, skin
    Novafil Polybutester Mono Slight elasticity; good vascular Skin closure, vascular
    Mersilene / Dacron Polyester Braided High tensile; low tissue reaction Cardiac, valve, tendon
    Ethibond Coated polyester Braided Better handling than uncoated; high tensile Cardiac, orthopedic, tendon
    Stainless steel wire Stainless steel Mono/multi Maximum tensile strength; permanent Sternal closure, bone
    Ti-cron Polyester Braided Similar to Ethibond Cardiac, orthopedic

    Suture Sizing Reference¶

    Size Diameter Approximate Typical Use
    #5 0.7 mm Heavy orthopedic, wire equivalent
    #2 0.5 mm Tendon, heavy fascial
    #1 0.4 mm Fascial retention, mass closure
    #0 (0) 0.35 mm Fascia
    2-0 0.3 mm Deep fascial layers
    3-0 0.2 mm Subcutaneous, muscle, bowel
    4-0 0.15 mm Skin, vascular
    5-0 0.1 mm Vascular anastomosis, delicate skin
    6-0 0.07 mm Fine vascular, ophthalmology
    7-0 0.05 mm Microsurgery, cardiac anastomosis
    8-0 0.04 mm Microsurgery, corneal
    9-0 to 11-0 <0.04 mm Ophthalmic microsurgery

    Needle Type Quick Reference¶

    Point Type Symbol/Code Cross-Section Use
    Taper (round) RB, SH, MH, CT Round Soft tissue: bowel, fascia, vessel
    Conventional cutting CE Triangular (cutting edge inside curve) Skin, tough tissue
    Reverse cutting CE-2, PS, FS Triangular (cutting edge outside curve) Skin, fibrous tissue (preferred)
    Tapercut TC Taper body + cutting tip Calcified tissue, fascia
    Blunt BL Blunt rounded Liver, kidney, blunt dissection
    Spatula Flat with cutting edges Ophthalmic, superficial layers

    Common Needle Code Examples¶

    Code Description
    FS Free swaged (conventional cutting)
    FS-2 Free swaged-2 (smaller, conventional cutting)
    PS Plastic surgery (reverse cutting)
    PS-2 Plastic surgery-2 (smaller)
    SH Small half (taper; 1/2 circle; small)
    MH Medium half (taper; 1/2 circle; medium)
    CT Circle taper
    RB Round body (taper; 3/8 circle)
    KS Keith straight (straight cutting)
    TF Taper flushed; used for delicate tissues
